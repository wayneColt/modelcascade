  local:
    type: ollama
    model: llama3.2:3b
    cost_per_1k: 0.0

  fast:
    type: anthropic
    api_key: ${ANTHROPIC_API_KEY}
    model: claude-haiku-4-5-20251001
    cost_per_1k: 0.001

  capable:
    type: anthropic
    api_key: ${ANTHROPIC_API_KEY}
    model: claude-sonnet-4-6
    cost_per_1k: 0.005

routing:
  cost_ceiling: 0.01        # per request, hard stop
  cascade_on_failure: true
  calibration: preset_v1  # production-calibrated
  escalation: quality_or_timeout

04 Design principles
01 · CLASSIFICATION
Classify first,
spend second
Every request gets a difficulty score before a provider is chosen. The score drives the routing. The routing drives the cost. Not the other way around.
02 · ESCALATION
Fail cheap,
succeed capable
When a lower tier fails — quality threshold not met, timeout, rate limit — the executor escalates automatically to the next capable model. No manual intervention.
03 · OWNERSHIP
Your keys.
Your data.
modelcascade never routes through our servers. Your API keys stay on your machine. The calibration tables are yours to inspect and override. No telemetry.

Talk to Us
What happens if I hit my request limit?

Nothing breaks. Your requests keep routing. We notify you at 80% and 100%. Overage is billed at the same per-request rate — no surprise multipliers. You can also set a hard cap: "never exceed $X/mo" and we stop routing to paid models when you hit it.

What happens to my requests at 3 AM?

They get cheaper. Overnight routing shifts batch requests to the lowest-cost models — typically 95% local routing vs 74% during peak hours. Your morning report shows exactly what ran, which models were used, and what it cost. You review, not relay.

Is this just another API proxy?

No. ModelCascade is open source (MIT). The routing engine runs in your environment. We never see your prompts and never proxy your API calls. Paid tiers add managed keys, analytics, and support — not routing capability. You can leave anytime and keep the same pip package running.

My team is 3 people. Do I need the Scale tier?

Probably not yet. Start with Pro. When you want a shared dashboard or need cost-per-project tags for your manager's quarterly review, upgrade. Scale exists for organizational accountability, not technical capability.
